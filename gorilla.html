GORILLA

Gorillas belong to the family Hominidae, which includes humans, and are divided into two species: the Eastern gorilla (Gorilla beringei) and the Western gorilla (Gorilla gorilla). Each species is further subdivided into two subspecies: the Eastern gorilla consists of the Eastern lowland gorilla and the Mountain gorilla, while the Western gorilla consists of the Western lowland gorilla and the Cross River gorilla.

Gorillas have robust bodies with broad chests and shoulders, covered in coarse black hair. Adult males, called silverbacks due to the silver-gray hair that develops on their backs with age, can weigh up to 400 pounds (180 kilograms) and stand around 5 to 6 feet tall (1.5 to 1.8 meters). Females are typically smaller, weighing around half as much as males.

Gorillas are native to the dense forests and jungles of central Africa. They primarily inhabit montane, lowland, and swamp forests, where they forage for food and build nests for sleeping.
